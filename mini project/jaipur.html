Jaipur, famously known as the "Pink City", is the capital of Rajasthan and one of India’s most iconic tourist destinations. It’s a city that effortlessly blends rich history, royal architecture, vibrant culture, and modern development. The old city, painted entirely in shades of pink, was designed to welcome Prince Albert in 1876 and has since become a signature look for Jaipur. Walking through its streets feels like stepping into a living museum — from bustling bazaars and palaces to folk musicians and colorful textiles everywhere. Jaipur is also part of the Golden Triangle tourist circuit, along with Delhi and Agra, making it a must-visit spot for first-time travelers to India






Jaipur

The city is home to some of the most stunning architecture in the country. The Amber Fort, with its massive ramparts, mirrored halls, and elephant rides, is a masterpiece of Rajput architecture. The City Palace sits in the heart of Jaipur and offers a mix of Mughal and Rajasthani design with museums, courtyards, and royal halls. Then there’s the Hawa Mahal, or “Palace of Winds,” an iconic five-story structure with 953 tiny windows, built so royal women could observe street life without being seen. Jantar Mantar, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an ancient observatory that shows the scientific advancements of the time through massive astronomical instruments




MORE PLACES WORTH VISTING IN JAIPUR AS A TOURIST


Hawa Mahal

Probably the most iconic building in Jaipur. This pink, honeycomb-like structure has 953 small windows (jharokhas) built for royal ladies to observe street life without being seen. The view from the top is great for seeing the old city bustle.





Jal Mahal

Jal Mahal, literally meaning "Water Palace", is one of the most beautiful and serene spots in Jaipur. It sits right in the middle of Man Sagar Lake, surrounded by the Aravalli Hills, and looks straight-up magical, especially during sunrise or sunset
