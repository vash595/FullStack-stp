Bikaner is a city in the northwest of the state of Rajasthan, India. It is located 330 kilometres (205 mi) northwest of the state capital, Jaipur. It is the administrative headquarters of Bikaner District and Bikaner division. Formerly the capital of the princely Bikaner State, the city was founded by Rao Bika, a Rajput chief of the Rathore dynasty in 1488 CE[8][9] and from its small origins it has developed into the fourth largest city in Rajasthan. The Ganga Canal, completed in 1928, and the Indira Gandhi Canal, completed in 1987, facilitated its development.






Bikaner is a city

of those rare places in Rajasthan that gives you all the royal vibes and desert adventures without the typical tourist chaos. It’s tucked away in the northwestern part of the state, close to the Thar Desert, and offers a perfect mix of culture, history, architecture, and food. Unlike the more hyped destinations like Jaipur or Jodhpur, Bikaner feels more raw and authentic — like you’re walking through a city that hasn’t been overly commercialized yet. The city is home to the magnificent Junagarh Fort, one of the few forts in Rajasthan that isn’t built on a hill.


It’s got jaw-dropping interiors, beautiful courtyards, and collections of weapons, art, and ancient artifacts that show how powerful the Bikaner royals were. Close by is the Lalgarh Palace, which is another example of regal architecture, mixing Rajput, Mughal, and even European styles. It now functions partly as a museum and a heritage hotel, so even if you’re not staying there, it’s still worth a visit just to soak in the architecture.





MORE PLACES WORTH VISTING IN BIKANER AS A TOURIST


Junagarh Fort

Karni Mata Temple

One of the most unique (and honestly wild) places in Bikaner is the Karni Mata Temple, also known as the Rat Temple. Yeah — actual rats roam around here freely and are worshipped. It might sound creepy, but it’s considered sacred, and people believe it brings good luck if a rat runs over your foot. Another cool place is the National Research Centre on Camels, the only camel research institute in Asia. You can ride camels, learn about different breeds, and even try camel milk ice cream or kulfi. It's a desert vibe you won’t get anywhere else
