Ajmer, located in the heart of Rajasthan, is a city that blends spirituality, history, and culture in a unique way. It’s most famous for the Ajmer Sharif Dargah, the tomb of the Sufi saint Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti, which draws millions of pilgrims from all religions every year. The spiritual energy around the Dargah is intense, and the streets leading up to it are always buzzing with qawwalis, the smell of rose petals, and the sound of prayer. People believe that wishes made here come true, and the peaceful atmosphere around the Dargah makes it one of the most visited spiritual sites in India.

MORE PLACES WORTH VISTING IN AJMER AS A TOURIST


Ajmer Sharif Dargah

This is the main attraction in Ajmer — the shrine of Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti, a revered Sufi saint. People from all religions visit here to pray, tie a thread for wishes, and feel the peaceful energy of the place. The qawwalis and spiritual vibe hit different, especially on Fridays or during Urs (death anniversary of the saint).





Ana Sagar Lake

A beautiful artificial lake built in the 12th century, perfect for chilling by the water or catching a sunset. There’s boating, a walking area, and the surrounding Daulat Bagh gardens with marble pavilions add a Mughal touch. It’s a great spot to relax away from the city noise.
